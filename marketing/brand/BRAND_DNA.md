# SequenceFlow Brand DNA

Version: 2.0
Updated: June 13, 2026
Scope: SequenceFlow AI email support for e-commerce

## Core identity

**Category:** controlled AI customer service for email.
**Primary market:** Dutch e-commerce companies and online retailers.
**One-line position:** SequenceFlow turns a busy support mailbox into a controlled AI workflow that drafts answers from the company’s own policy while the team stays in charge.

SequenceFlow is not a generic chatbot, a replacement helpdesk, an automation agency, or fully autonomous customer service. It is a focused layer for receiving, understanding, drafting, reviewing, and sending support email.

## Mission

Give small and growing e-commerce teams the response capacity of a larger support operation without forcing them to surrender quality, policy, or brand voice.

## Customer promise

**Get through recurring customer email faster, with answers grounded in your own rules and a human control point where it matters.**

## Product truth

SequenceFlow can currently:

- receive email through forwarding or IMAP;
- send through a configured SMTP mailbox;
- classify intent and estimate confidence;
- generate a reply in the customer’s language;
- use uploaded policy and knowledge documents as context;
- let an agent review, edit, schedule, approve, send, ignore, or escalate;
- offer controlled auto-send on Pro and higher;
- show volume, intent, outcome, and pain-point analytics;
- support admins and agents inside one tenant.

Default behaviour is human approval. This is a feature, not an apology.

## Positioning pillars

### 1. Control before autonomy

AI prepares the work. The customer decides when automation earns more freedom. Confidence thresholds, review, scheduling, and escalation make that visible.

### 2. Your policy is the source

Returns, delivery, warranty, product, and tone-of-voice documents shape the answer. We sell fewer generic AI guesses, not “magic”.

### 3. Works with the mailbox you have

The product connects through forwarding or IMAP and sends through SMTP. Customers do not need to migrate their entire support operation to test value.

### 4. Built for recurring e-commerce questions

Order status, returns, damaged products, invoices, delivery expectations, and product questions are the sharp initial use case.

## Reasons to believe

- 14-day trial, 150 processed emails, no credit card.
- Review-first onboarding.
- Knowledge documents are visible and manageable by the customer.
- Intent and confidence are shown with the draft.
- One support mailbox keeps setup and pricing understandable.
- Trial, plan, member, document, and email limits are enforced in the product.

## Personality

Calm, capable, specific, transparent, and operational. SequenceFlow should sound like the experienced colleague who has already read the policy, not a breathless AI keynote.

### Voice rules

- Lead with the customer’s work, then explain the AI.
- Use concrete verbs: reads, classifies, drafts, checks, reviews, sends.
- Say “controlled auto-send,” not “full autopilot.”
- Prefer “support mailbox” over “omnichannel customer experience platform.”
- Explain constraints plainly.
- Use short Dutch sentences and familiar e-commerce language.
- Never shame a founder or agent for doing manual work.

### Words to use

`supportmailbox`, `conceptantwoord`, `eigen beleid`, `goedkeuring`, `vertrouwen`, `kennisbank`, `gecontroleerde auto-send`, `klantmail`, `retour`, `bestelstatus`.

### Words to avoid

`magisch`, `menselijke medewerkers vervangen`, `100% autonoom`, `foutloos`, `instant ROI`, `AI operating system`, `nooit meer support`, `binnen één middag live`.

## Claim guardrails

Allowed without extra evidence:

- product capabilities listed above;
- published plan limits and prices;
- “no credit card required” and the 14-day trial;
- “not used to train OpenAI models” when linked to current API policy;
- qualitative outcomes such as less repetitive copying or more consistency.

Require measured customer evidence before use:

- time saved, percentage automated, response-time improvement;
- conversion, retention, CSAT, revenue, or cost reduction;
- setup time promises;
- customer counts, ratings, or “most chosen” claims based on real data.

Never claim:

- zero errors, guaranteed compliance, guaranteed savings;
- that staff can never access production data;
- certifications or legal compliance not formally obtained;
- integrations not present in the product.

## Visual DNA

### Palette

- Ink: `#111711`
- Deep background: `#0D1117`
- Lime signal: `#C7F56F`
- Warm canvas: `#F7F7F2`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Muted text: `#667064`
- Blue status: `#60A5FA`

Lime is a signal for progress, confidence, and primary actions. It should not fill every surface. Dark ink and warm off-white carry the brand.

### Typography and layout

- Use Geist/Inter/system sans serif.
- Headlines are compact, sentence case, and slightly tight.
- UI proof should resemble a real support workflow, not abstract AI imagery.
- Use generous whitespace, 12–20 px radii, thin borders, and restrained shadows.
- Creative hierarchy: pain or outcome first, product proof second, trial CTA third.

## Message hierarchy

1. **Problem:** recurring customer email consumes attention and creates a queue.
2. **Mechanism:** AI drafts from the company’s own policy.
3. **Control:** the team reviews first and automates later.
4. **Low-risk start:** existing mailbox, 14 days, no card.
5. **Action:** start the trial.
