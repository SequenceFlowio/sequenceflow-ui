# Persona 3: E-commerce operations manager

## Snapshot

Owns cross-functional efficiency across support, fulfilment, and online operations. Often influences the purchase and needs a clear business case for the founder or management team.

## Job to be done

“Make recurring support work scalable and reveal which operational issues create avoidable contact.”

## Triggers

- Order growth outpaces support capacity.
- Shipping or return issues create repetitive contact.
- Hiring is the only proposed answer to each peak.
- Leadership lacks insight into why customers contact support.

## Pain and emotion

- Cost per order rises with support volume.
- Operational defects stay hidden inside the inbox.
- Tool sprawl and migrations slow improvement.
- Automation projects fail because ownership is unclear.

## Buying criteria

Low-risk rollout, mailbox compatibility, measurable volume and intent, clear plan limits, role ownership, retained human control.

## Objections and responses

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| “We already have a mailbox/helpdesk.” | SequenceFlow can be tested with the existing mailbox rather than replacing the full stack. |
| “Show me the ROI.” | Establish a baseline for volume, handle time, backlog, and repeat contacts before making savings claims. |
| “This could create another silo.” | Use pain-point analytics as input for fulfilment, policy, and product changes. |
| “Who owns errors?” | Approval remains explicit; auto-send is a controlled setting and the customer remains responsible for sent content. |

## Best message

“Scale recurring support and turn inbox patterns into operational signals, without a helpdesk migration.”
