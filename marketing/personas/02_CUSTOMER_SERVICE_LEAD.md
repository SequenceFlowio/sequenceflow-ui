# Persona 2: Customer-service lead

## Snapshot

Leads 2–10 agents at a growing e-commerce company. Accountable for queue health, answer quality, onboarding, escalations, and consistency.

## Job to be done

“Increase team capacity and consistency without losing oversight of exceptions or brand-sensitive replies.”

## Triggers

- First-response time deteriorates during peaks.
- Agents repeatedly search for policy or rewrite standard answers.
- Answers differ between team members.
- Management asks for automation but the lead carries quality risk.

## Pain and emotion

- Queue pressure and uneven workloads.
- Policy updates do not reach every answer.
- Black-box automation creates anxiety.
- Reporting explains volume but not recurring customer problems.

## Buying criteria

Roles, shared knowledge, edit/approve workflow, confidence visibility, escalations, controlled auto-send, analytics, predictable limits.

## Objections and responses

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| “This sidelines my agents.” | Agents remain the control point and spend more attention on exceptions. |
| “We cannot risk an incorrect auto-reply.” | Auto-send is off by default and can be limited by confidence and schedule. |
| “Our policy changes.” | Admins manage the knowledge documents used for drafts. |
| “We need auditability.” | The workflow retains status, decision, and outcome metadata while minimising content in event logs. |

## Best message

“Give every agent the same policy-aware starting point, while sensitive and uncertain replies stay visible for review.”
