# Persona 1: E-commerce founder

## Snapshot

Owner or operator of a Dutch webshop with roughly 1–10 employees. Support is still partly handled by the founder, often between fulfilment, marketing, purchasing, and finance.

## Job to be done

“Help me stop revisiting the inbox all day while keeping customer answers trustworthy and recognisably ours.”

## Triggers

- Campaigns or seasonal peaks create a visible backlog.
- The same order-status and return questions recur every day.
- A first support hire is being considered but volume is inconsistent.
- The founder worries that slow answers harm reviews or repeat purchases.

## Pain and emotion

- Constant context switching.
- Guilt when replies wait.
- Fear of an AI making an expensive promise or refund decision.
- Frustration with tools that require a migration project.

## Buying criteria

Fast trial, no credit card, works with the existing mailbox, answers use their own policy, manual review by default, understandable monthly price.

## Objections and responses

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| “AI will invent things.” | Drafts use uploaded policy and begin in review mode. |
| “I do not have time for implementation.” | The onboarding is a short sequence: mailbox, knowledge, sender test. Do not promise a fixed setup time. |
| “We are too small.” | Starter is designed for one mailbox, two users, and 250 emails per month. |
| “I can just use ChatGPT.” | SequenceFlow combines inbox context, policy, workflow, sending, and analytics rather than requiring copy-paste per message. |

## Best message

“Stop answering every recurring customer email between your other work. SequenceFlow prepares a policy-aware reply; you decide what needs you.”
